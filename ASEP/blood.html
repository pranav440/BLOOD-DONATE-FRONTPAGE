🩸 Know Your Blood Type

What Are Blood Types?

Blood types are categorized into four main groups: A, B, AB, and O. Each group is further classified as positive (+) or negative (−) based on the Rh factor, resulting in eight blood types. Knowing your blood type is essential for safe blood transfusions and medical emergencies.

Universal Donors & Recipients

O− is the universal donor, meaning it can be given to any blood type. Conversely, AB+ is the universal recipient, able to receive blood from any group. This knowledge is especially vital in emergency situations.

Interesting Blood Facts

  • Only 1 in 15 people have O− blood.
  • AB− is the rarest blood type.
  • You can donate blood every 3 months (males) or 4 months (females).
  • One donation can save up to 3 lives!
  • Blood makes up about 7% of your body weight.

✅ Frequently Asked Questions

Is donating blood safe?

Yes, donating blood is completely safe. All equipment used is sterile and single-use to prevent any infection or health risk.

How often can I donate blood?

Generally, males can donate every 3 months, and females every 4 months. This allows your body enough time to replenish the blood.

Who can donate blood?

Anyone aged between 18 to 65 years, weighing at least 50 kg, and in good health can donate blood. However, specific eligibility criteria may vary slightly.

Is there a record of my donations?

Yes, your donations are tracked and recorded in your profile. You can also receive digital certificates as proof of your contributions.

Do I need to pay to use this service?

No, the platform is free to use. Our goal is to help save lives through voluntary blood donation.

What should I do before donating blood?

Ensure you are well-hydrated, have eaten a healthy meal, and are not feeling sick or fatigued. Avoid alcohol and smoking before donating.

Can I donate blood if I have a medical condition?

It depends on the condition. Consult with your doctor or the blood donation center to determine your eligibility.

Will donating blood make me weak?

No, your body quickly replenishes the lost blood. You may feel slightly tired, but this can be managed by staying hydrated and resting.

How long does the donation process take?

The entire process, including registration, donation, and post-donation rest, usually takes about 1-2 hours.

Can I donate blood if I am on medication?

Some medications may affect your eligibility. Always consult with your healthcare provider or the donation center before donating.

How is my blood type determined?

Your blood type is determined by the presence or absence of specific antigens on the surface of your red blood cells. These antigens are inherited from your parents and are classified into the ABO and Rh systems.

What are the benefits of donating blood?

Donating blood helps save lives, improves your cardiovascular health, and provides a free mini-health check-up.

How much blood is taken during donation?

Typically, about 1 pint (450 ml) of blood is collected during a donation.
